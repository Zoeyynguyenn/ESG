## Snapshot

Page title:

Raytheon Company to Pay Over $950M in Connection with Defective Pricing, Foreign Bribery, and Export Control Schemes.

Date:

Wednesday, October 16, 2024.

Core content:

The U.S. Department of Justice states that Raytheon Company, a subsidiary of RTX, will pay over $950 million to resolve investigations into:

1. a major government fraud scheme involving defective pricing on certain government contracts
2. violations of the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act
3. violations of the Arms Export Control Act and related export control regulations

According to the press release:

- Raytheon will enter into deferred prosecution agreements.
- Raytheon is required to retain an independent compliance monitor for three years.
- Raytheon must enhance its internal compliance program.
- Raytheon must report evidence of additional misconduct and cooperate in ongoing or future criminal investigations.

Additional details in the snapshot:

- The DOJ states that Raytheon employees provided false and fraudulent information to the Department of Defense during contract negotiations.
- The schemes allegedly caused the Department of Defense to pay Raytheon over $111 million more than it should have paid.
- The release also references a separate False Claims Act settlement and related compliance remediation measures.
